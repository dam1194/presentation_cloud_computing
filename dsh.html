What We All Know

Let's start with what we already know from our courses:

Asymmetric Crypto

RSA & ECC: The magic handshake

  • Uses public key (lock) to encrypt
  • Uses private key (secret key) to decrypt
  • Secures TLS/SSL connections, emails, digital signatures
Symmetric Crypto

AES: Fast and strong for bulk encryption

  • Used after the handshake
  • Encrypts the actual data
  • Not directly broken by quantum computers

The Problem: RSA and ECC security relies on hard math problems (factoring huge numbers) that are easy for quantum computers with Shor's algorithm.

The Heroes Enter

Enter the heroes: Quantum-Safe Cryptography (also called Post-Quantum Cryptography - PQC).

These are new cryptographic algorithms designed to run on the computers and phones we have today, but whose security is based on math problems that are hard for both classical AND quantum computers.

They are the new, future-proof locks for our cloud vaults.

How Do These New Locks Work?

The cool part! NIST is running a competition to pick the best QSC algorithms. They rely on different, harder puzzles:

Lattice-Based
"Finding a needle in a multidimensional haystack." Security based on the difficulty of finding the shortest point in a complex lattice. (Leading candidate)
Code-Based
"Correcting giant, noisy codes." Based on the difficulty of decoding a random linear code. Been around for decades and considered very strong.
Hash-Based
"Chaining hashes together." Uses the security of hash functions (like SHA-256) to build digital signatures. Very conservative and trusted.
Others
Multivariate & Isogeny-Based: Other fascinating mathematical approaches being researched and tested in the NIST competition.

NIST Competition: The National Institute of Standards and Technology is currently finalizing standards for quantum-safe cryptography, with winners announced in 2022-2024.

What This Means for the Cloud

Migrating the cloud to QSC isn't just a software update. It's a major shift:

Legacy Cloud
Crypto-Agility
Quantum-Safe Cloud
Crypto-Agility

The #1 concept for cloud architects. It means designing systems now so we can easily swap out cryptographic algorithms later.

Think of it as building a lock with interchangeable cores.

Hybrid Cryptography

The smart transition path. For now, we can use both traditional RSA and a quantum-safe algorithm together.

This protects against both current and future threats during the transition.

The Challenge: Larger key sizes and signatures mean more bandwidth and slightly slower handshakes. This affects every TLS connection, VPN, and software update in the cloud.
